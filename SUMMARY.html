2,000 Step LLM Speedrun Submission

Final Result

  • Best Dev BPB: 2.2612
  • Total Parameters: 1,340,864
  • Training Steps: 2000
  • Framework: PyTorch (CPU)

Architecture

The final model retains the original Transformer backbone while replacing the standard feed-forward network with a SwiGLU block. The optimizer is AdamW with cosine learning-rate scheduling, 200-step warmup, weight decay of 0.05 and gradient clipping.

Experiments

Run Modification Dev BPB
1 Baseline 2.5101
2 Larger Transformer 2.6433
3 SwiGLU + AdamW + Cosine LR + Warmup 2.2612
4 Xavier Initialization + Parameter Groups 2.43

Key Findings

  • Increasing model size under a fixed 2000-step budget reduced performance.
  • SwiGLU provided the largest improvement while staying within the parameter budget.
  • Optimization quality had a larger impact than increasing model capacity.
  • The final checkpoint achieved the lowest development BPB among all tested configurations.

Human vs AI Contribution

AI assistance was used for discussing optimization ideas, debugging environment setup, and reviewing implementation options. The experimental design, implementation, execution of training runs, evaluation, comparison of results, and final model selection were performed by the author based on measured performance.
